Study on the influence of bond-slip behavior on the bending performance of reinforced recycled aggregate concrete beams

关键结果

  • 明确当粘结强度损失超过 40% 时，会对梁的受弯性能产生显著影响。
  • 200 次冻融循环后，梁的开裂、屈服、极限荷载分别下降 32%、9.4%、9.3%。
  • 揭示开裂后梁轴向粘结应力的波动规律，明确裂缝位置会出现粘结应力最小值。

Abstract

Bond is essential for the collaboration between rebar and recycled aggregate concrete (RAC). Numerous bond theories have been proposed; however, the implications on the flexural performance of beams have not been definitively elucidated. Firstly, the bond theories were summarized, and the differences were compared. Secondly, the 3D finite element model of reinforced recycled aggregate concrete (RRAC) beams, considering bond, was established. The influences of different bond theories on beam were analyzed.

Subsequently, the bending performance of RRAC beams considering bond damage after freeze-thaw cycles was studied. Finally, the stress along the axis direction at different load stages were studied. The results indicate that the bond theories differ significantly, but their impact on beam flexural behavior is relatively small; once bond strength loss exceeds 40%, the beam is significantly affected; after 200 freeze-thaw cycles, the cracking, yield, and ultimate load decrease by 32%, 9.4%, and 9.3%, respectively.
